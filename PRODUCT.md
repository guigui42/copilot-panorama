# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Developers, team leads, and decision-makers evaluating or adopting GitHub Copilot. They arrive curious but unsure of the full surface area — what Copilot is, where it lives (CLI, IDE, Cloud), and how the `.github/` composable stack fits together. Context is exploratory: skimming on a laptop, often linked from docs or social, wanting a fast mental model rather than reference detail.

## Product Purpose

An interactive visual guide that maps *everything GitHub Copilot can do* across two views — the `.github/` composable stack and "Copilot Everywhere" surfaces. It exists to turn a sprawling, fast-moving product into one legible picture. Success is the "now I get it" moment: a visitor leaves with an accurate mental model of the layers and where to go next, in multiple languages.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, clear, approachable. Voice is a knowledgeable guide, not a marketer — explains rather than sells. Visuals do the heavy lifting: the interface should feel easy to read and easy to scan, lowering the barrier to understanding a complex system. Confident but never dense or intimidating.

## Anti-references

- Dense enterprise documentation walls (text-heavy, no visual hierarchy).
- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient-text heroes, identical icon-card grids, per-section uppercase eyebrows.
- Overstimulating, gimmicky motion that distracts from comprehension.

## Design Principles

- **Show, don't tell.** Comprehension comes from the visual structure, not paragraphs.
- **Legibility first.** Every layer, label, and state must be instantly readable; clarity beats cleverness.
- **Earn the interaction.** Clicks, panels, and motion must teach something, not just decorate.
- **Inclusive by default.** Works across languages, themes, input methods, and reduced-motion needs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: ≥4.5:1 body contrast, visible focus states, keyboard-operable cards/panels/switchers, and a working focus trap in the detail modal. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` on every animation. Five locales (en, fr, es, de, ja) must all stay legible and on-brand.
